About

Hi, I'm Mati Dashkov, an 18-year-old Israeli pre-university student passionate about embedded systems and hardware design. This blog documents my journey from self-taught maker to future Electrical & Computer Engineering student at Technion.

My Journey​

I started tinkering with electronics on breadboards, learning through trial and error, datasheets, and online resources. What began as curiosity about how things work has evolved into a serious pursuit of professional hardware design. I'm building in public to share what I learn, document my mistakes, and connect with others on similar paths.

Current Focus​

Right now, I'm deep into the eduLAB Synthesis Engine project - a high-performance audio synthesis engine running on Teensy 4.1. This project combines:

  • Real-time DSP programming in C++
  • Mixed-signal hardware design
  • I2S audio interfaces
  • PCB design and layout

It's teaching me the practical challenges of embedded systems that you can't learn from textbooks alone.
